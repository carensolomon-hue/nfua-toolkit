What Is a Tax Allocation District?
A plain-language guide to how TADs work and what they mean for Atlanta neighborhoods.
The Basics
What Is a Tax Allocation District (TAD)?

A Tax Allocation District (TAD) is a defined geographic area where a portion of future growth in property tax revenue is reinvested to fund improvements within that same area.

It is a financing tool that allows the city to support neighborhood improvements by using growth that happens over time.

How It Works
The Three-Part Cycle
1
The Base

When a TAD is created, the existing property tax value is set as a baseline. The City of Atlanta, Atlanta Public Schools, and Fulton County continue receiving taxes on that base every year.

2
The Growth

As property values increase over time, the additional tax revenue generated by that growth is captured within the TAD.

3
The Reinvestment

That growth is reinvested back into the area to help fund projects like housing, infrastructure, small business support, and community facilities.

Community Impact
TADs and the Neighborhood Reinvestment Initiative

TADs are one of the tools used to support the Neighborhood Reinvestment Initiative (NRI), helping fund improvements in areas that have not seen consistent investment.

The goal is to support more complete, connected neighborhoods by funding projects that improve access to housing, services, and economic opportunity, while prioritizing stability for existing residents.

Key Information
Important Facts About Atlanta TADs
No Tax Increases

TADs do not raise tax rates. They use growth in property values over time to fund improvements.

Who Participates

Three taxing jurisdictions participate in Atlanta TADs:

  • City of Atlanta
  • Atlanta Public Schools (APS)
  • Fulton County
Oversight and Timeline

TADs are authorized under Georgia law and administered by Invest Atlanta. Each TAD has a defined boundary, a start date, and an expected end date. When a TAD ends, all property tax revenue, including the growth, returns fully to the general funds.
