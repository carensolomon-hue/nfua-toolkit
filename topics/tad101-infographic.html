How TAD Funding Works
A visual guide to Tax Allocation Districts and their impact on Atlanta neighborhoods
The Process
How a TAD Generates and Reinvests Funding
1
TAD Boundary Created
City designates an area. Existing property value is frozen as the "base."
›
2
Property Values Grow
Investment spurs development. Assessed values rise above the frozen base.
›
3
Increment Is Captured
The extra tax revenue from growth, the "increment," flows into a dedicated TAD fund.
›
4
Funds Reinvested
TAD funds pay for housing, infrastructure, small businesses, and more in the district.
›
5
TAD Closes
At end date, all tax revenue, at the full grown value, returns to the City, APS, and Fulton County.
Where the Money Goes
The Tax Increment Cycle
🏠
Property Owners Pay Taxes
All property owners in the TAD pay taxes as normal. No tax increase.
→
💵
Growth Above Base = Increment
The portion above the frozen base value is captured and held in the TAD fund.
→
🏛
Reinvested in the District
Grants, loans, and bonds fund affordable housing, infrastructure, and neighborhood services.
